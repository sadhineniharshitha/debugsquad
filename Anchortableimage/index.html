Seven Wonders Of World

Great Wall Of China


The Great wall of china is one of the most awe-inspiring architectural feats in human history. Stretching over 21,000 kilometers across rugged mountains, deserts, and grasslands, it was originally built to protect ancient Chinese kingdoms from northern invasions.

Tajmahal


TheTaj Mahal ,located in Agra, India, is a breathtaking monument built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631 during childbirth. Construction began in 1632 and took over two decades to complete, involving more than 20,000 artisans under the guidance of architect Ustad Ahmad Lahori

petra

Petra is a mesmerizing ancient city carved into the rose-tinted cliffs of southern Jordan, where history whispers through every stone. Hidden behind a winding gorge known as the Siq, this once-thriving Nabataean capital dazzles visitors with its monumental architecture and ingenious engineering.

machu picchu

Machu Picchu is a breathtaking 15th-century Inca citadel perched high in the Andes Mountains of southern Peru, at an elevation of about 2,430 meters (7,970 feet). Often called the “Lost City of the Incas,” it was built during the reign of Emperor Pachacuti and is believed to have served as a royal retreat or sacred ceremonial site

chichen itza

Chichen Itza is one of the most iconic archaeological sites of the ancient Maya civilization, located in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico. Flourishing between the 9th and 13th centuries, it served as a major political, religious, and cultural center. The name“Chichén Itzá” translates to “At the mouth of the well of the Itza,” referring to the nearby sacred cenotes—natural sinkholes that provided water and were used for ceremonial offerings.

Colosseum

The Colosseum ,also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is one of the most iconic landmarks of ancient Rome and a symbol of the grandeur of the Roman Empire. Built between 70–80 CE under the emperors Vespasian and Titus, this massive elliptical structure could hold between 50,000 to 80,000 spectators

christ the redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is one of the most iconic monuments in the world, standing tall atop Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Completed in 1931, this colossal Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ stretches 30 meters (98 feet) high, with arms spanning 28 meters (92 feet) wide
